BirbCafe Admin Guide

BirbCafe.com Store Administrator Guide

Welcome to the official user guide for operating and managing BirbCafe.com! This document focuses on the administrative functions of your WooCommerce dashboard, providing detailed guidance for staff and owners.

This section provides a guide to the WooCommerce administrative dashboard for store management.

‚öôÔ∏è Store Management

This section contains quick links to crucial setup and management areas:

Marketing & Merchandising

  • Marketing: Access a page where you can find extensions (add-ons) to enhance your store's marketing capabilities.
  • Add products: Quickly jump to the product creation screen to list new items.
  • Personalize my store: Allows for basic customizations of the store's appearance, though this feature may be bypassed or offer limited utility if you are using a detailed, custom theme.
  • View my store: Opens the public-facing store in a new tab to see what customers see.

Settings

  • Store details: Update your store's name, address, currency, and other general information.
  • Payments: Configure and manage the payment gateways (like PayPal or Stripe) your store accepts.
  • Tax: Set up and manage tax rates and options for different regions.
  • Shipping: Define shipping zones, methods, and costs for delivering products to customers.

üõí Section B: Managing Orders

The Orders section is the main hub for processing, tracking, and completing customer purchases.

1. The Orders Screen Overview

This section lists all transactions and allows you to manage them efficiently.

  • Add order: Button at the top to manually create a new order (e.g., for phone orders). Note: This feature is currently not being used.
  • Order Status Filters: Quick-access tabs above the main list allow filtering by the current state of the order:
    • All (22): Shows every order.
    • Pending payment (3): The customer has initiated the order but payment has not been successfully received.
    • Completed (18): The order has been paid for and successfully delivered/fulfilled.
    • Failed (1): Payment failed or the order was cancelled by the system.
  • Search Orders: Use the search bar on the right to find specific orders by customer name, order number, or total amount.

2. Filtering and Sorting Orders

The row of dropdown menus helps you quickly refine the list to find specific sets of orders:

  • Bulk actions: Allows you to select multiple orders (using the checkboxes on the left) and apply status changes or management actions simultaneously. Common actions include:
    • Change status to processing/on-hold/completed/cancelled.
    • Move to Trash.
    • Send/Cancel Review Reminder. 
    • Click Apply after selecting an action.
  • Filter by date (All dates): Restrict the visible orders to a specific time range by selecting a date range from the dropdown.
  • Filter by shipping provider: Filter orders based on the carrier used. Available options include: DHL US, FedEx, UPS, USPS, and FedEx Ground.
  • Filter by order type (All orders types): This filter is essential for subscription stores and allows you to narrow results by transaction type, including:
    • Original: The initial purchase of a product or subscription.
    • Subscription Parent: The main, recurring order record.
    • Subscription Renewal: Automatic recurring charges for an active subscription.
    • Subscription Resubscribe/Switch: Orders generated when a customer restarts or changes their plan.
    • Non-subscription: Standard one-time purchases.
  • Filter by sales channel (All sales channels): Filter orders based on the specific origin of the purchase. The available options are:
    • Admin: Orders that were manually created by a staff member via the WooCommerce dashboard.
    • Checkout: Orders placed by a customer directly through the BirbCafe.com website.
    • Point of Sale: Orders processed using a dedicated retail system (if applicable).
  • Filter by registered customer: Filter to see orders only from customers who have created an account. This is useful for quickly identifying orders that may need manual customer service follow-up.
  • Sorting: You can sort the orders in ascending or descending order by clicking on the column headers for Order #, Date, and Total. An arrow will indicate the active sorting direction.

3. Order Details Columns

The table provides essential information at a glance:

To view or edit the items ordered, billing address, shipping address, and order notes, click the Order # link.

4. Detailed Order Edit Screen

Clicking the Order # link on the main table opens the detailed Order Edit screen, which is used for fulfillment, tracking, and management.

Order Information Area (Top Left)

This area summarizes the general and payment details for Order #11424 (using the sample image).

  • Order Header: Displays the unique Order # and the initial summary of the order placement time and customer IP.
  • General Panel: Contains core administrative details:
    • Date created: The exact date and time the order was placed.
    • Status: A dropdown menu allowing immediate status changes (e.g., from Processing to Completed).
    • Customer: Links to the customer's account profile and history.
  • CryptoPay Payment Details: This specific panel appears when payment is processed via CryptoPay and contains transaction metadata:
    • Blockchain Network: The network used for the transaction (e.g., Base).
    • Payment Amount: The exact amount paid in the cryptocurrency's unit (e.g., BIRB).
    • Transaction Hash: The unique on-chain identifier for the transaction.

Billing and Shipping (Center Columns)

This area contains the customer's contact and address information:

  • Billing: The name, address, and contact information (email, phone) used for payment.
  • Shipping: The name, address, and contact information used for delivery.

Order Item Breakdown (Bottom Center)

This table lists all products purchased, shipping details, applied coupons, and the final cost breakdown:

  • Item: Lists the product name, SKU, price, and quantity ordered.
  • Shipping: Displays the shipping option chosen by the customer (e.g., UPS Ground), along with any internal service information and the cost paid by the customer for delivery (e.g., $13.95).
  • Coupons: Any discount codes or gift certificates applied to the order (e.g., bd71b0ss).
  • Order Totals: A breakdown of the cost: Items Subtotal, Coupon(s), Shipping, Order Total, and the amount Paid.

Side Panel Management (Right Column)

This panel contains actions and supplementary data related to the customer and fulfillment:

  • Order Actions: A dropdown to perform administrative actions such as:
    • Change status: Quickly set the order status (same as the main Status dropdown).
    • Email invoice/details: Manually send the order confirmation or invoice to the customer.
    • Move to trash: Remove the order from the active list.
    • Click Update after selecting an action.
  • Order Attribution: Shows the customer's origin (e.g., Direct) and device type (e.g., Mobile).
  • Customer History: Shows the total orders, total revenue, and average order value for this specific customer.
  • Shipment Tracking: Allows staff to Add Tracking Info by selecting a carrier (e.g., UPS Ground) and entering the tracking number. This is essential for communicating delivery details to the customer.
  • Affiliate Commission: Allows staff to select which affiliate (if any) should receive commission for this specific sale.
  • Order Notes: This dynamic section tracks the entire history of the order and allows staff to add communication.
    • System Notes: Automatic entries show internal changes (e.g., status updates, payment confirmations, stock changes, shipping label creation). These notes are for informational purposes and cannot be edited.
    • Private Notes (Internal Staff Only): These notes are visible only to store staff and are used for internal communication, reminders, or special instructions (e.g., "Customer called to confirm address"). To add a private note, type your comment in the Add note field, select Private note from the dropdown, and click Add.
    • Customer Notes: These notes are either left by the customer during checkout or are visible to the customer (if chosen by staff). They represent communication that the customer can see.
